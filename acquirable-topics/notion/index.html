Notion is a fantastic tool. It offers you countless functions to take notes, organise your data and much more. By now, I use it for organising almost everything.

On the other hand, the sheer amount of functions made it very difficult for me to get started. But this does not have to be this way. In this article, I will tell you how exactly I managed to master Notion.

30.03.2025

Amazing Flexibility

If I had to describe Notion in one word, it would be “flexible”. Notion has no clear structure, the structure is up to you and your needs. You can use it just to write down a few bullet points, but you can also use it to manage the internal processes of an entire company.

Notion has countless functions apart from bullet points you can use to your advantage. A few examples:

Pages, sub-pages, links to other Notion pages

Databases with different views, filters and sorting functions

Database entry templates

Buttons

Columns

Code blocks

Programming with formulas

Plug-ins from many other apps (e.g. Google Drive, Google Maps, GitHub, Slack, Figma and many more).

When I got started with Notion, almost none of these things said anything to me. I was extremely overwhelmed with all of those functions and the lack of a given structure.

As a result, I quit Notion about five times. For me, it was just a highly confusing note-taking app at that time.

Today, it is my favourite app - by far. This is what I use it for:

Taking notes

Organising my projects (yes, also Acquirable)

Storing my ideas

Tracking my habits

Tracking my goals

Journaling to write down my thoughts, emotions and values

Writing down what I have learnt

Saving useful websites and videos

This is the overview of my Notion journal. It is inspired by Jun Yuh’s 4 Win Notion Template.

Further Recommendations

Recently, I was scrolling on Pinterest when I found one of the most useful tools I have ever seen.

I often face the problem of finding an interesting website or an interesting video but having no time for it right now. As a result, those videos and websites often get lost.

The tool I found fixes this problem in an instant. It is a browser extension called Notion Web Clipper that allows me to save every website or video on Notion to visit or watch it later.

Now, I have a separate Notion page for all the useful and interesting websites and videos I have found to come back to.
