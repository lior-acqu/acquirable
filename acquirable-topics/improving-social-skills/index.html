Acquirable Title

Social skills are extremely important. They are the foundation of every interaction we have with people - and those interactions are the base of every relationship. Thus, social skills determine how good and meaningful our conversations, relationships and the opportunities we get from them are.

Since social skills are so important, it is in our best interest to improve them. In this article, we are going to look at the two main approaches.

The Theoretical Approach

The first approach to improving our social skills is by learning through media, books are especially books. Here are some of my book suggestions:

Crucial Conversations by Kerry Patterson, Joseph Grenny, Ron McMillan and Al Switzler

The 5 Love Languages by Gary Chapman

How to Talk to Anyone by Leil Lowndes

How to Win Friends and Influence People by Dale Carnegie

Of this list, my favourite book is How to Win Friends and Influence People. It provides a great start to your path of improvement, since it talks about improving your social skills in a very positive, encouraging manner.

Reading online articles (just like this one) is also a good option. Just by looking up "How to improve social skills", you will find excellent advice. Another option would be watching videos on YouTube about the topic.

The theoretical approach is fantastic for many reasons:

You can learn from absolute experts.

You decide how quickly you want to learn, there is no uncomfortable pressure.

You will not only learn about social skills, but about psychology in general, which is extremely valuable.

However, there are also some disadvantages:

You will still have to make an effort to apply the theory to real life.

What you read might not apply 100% to real life.

The process will take a long time.

The Practical Approach

Learning through media is great, but what if you want faster, more real-world-based results? In that case, you need a practical and intense approach. It comes with significantly more struggles, but in the end, it will be worth it.

The idea is to force yourself to social interactions until you have an intuition for them. But who are you going to talk with? Friends and family are a good option. You can even tell them about your motivation to become more socially skilled, so they can give you advice.

Talking to strangers on the streets is an even more intense option, especially because it is unpredictable and a bit scary. In some countries, though, this is a no-go and you might face a lot of rejection.

My favourite option is getting a job where you have to talk to a lot of strangers for at least one day per week. You could do this in sales, in a restaurant, as a receptionist, in a shop or at a bar. Such jobs force you to have hundreds of daily conversations with absolute strangers willing to talk to you.

This offers you countless opportunities to practice. You can train your confidence, talk in different styles to see how people react, try out new jokes, learn how to read people, and find good ways to deal with mistakes and awkward pauses. The amount of practice you get through such a job is invaluable. Pretty soon, talking to anyone will feel almost intuitive to you.

Acquired Topic: Improving Social Skills

Social skills are very important since they are the best way to get to know people, improve your existing relationships and get more opportunities.

Learning through media is great since you can learn at your own pace without pressure. You will also learn a lot about psychology as a bonus.

If you want quick results, my suggestion is getting a job where social interactions happen in large amounts on a daily basis.

For the best results, you can combine both the theoretical and the practical approach and become an experienced and knowledgeable speaker and listener. This is exactly what I did. I made remarkable improvements and was also having a lot fun.

No matter which approach you choose, you will benefit anyway. So, give it a try!
